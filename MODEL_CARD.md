# Model Card: First-Year Retention Risk

## Overview

A calibrated gradient-boosted classifier that estimates the probability a first-time-in-college (FTIC) student returns for their second year, based on demographics, academic preparation, financial aid, and first-year engagement signals.

This model is a **portfolio demonstration trained on fully synthetic data**. It is not deployed and should not be used to make real decisions about real students.

## Intended use

- **Primary use.** Surface students who may benefit from proactive advisor outreach during the first year.
- **Secondary use.** Cohort-level reporting on retention drivers for institutional planning.
- **Out of scope.** Admissions decisions, financial aid awards, conduct decisions, or any individual consequential decision without human review.

## Inputs

| Group | Features |
|---|---|
| Academic preparation | `hs_gpa`, `sat_total` |
| Program | `program_code` (one-hot) |
| Financial | `institutional_aid`, `federal_aid`, `unmet_need` |
| Context | `distance_from_home_mi` |
| First-year signals | `mean_term_gpa`, `total_credits_earned`, `mean_lms`, `total_advising`, `ever_on_campus` |

Protected attributes (gender, race/ethnicity, first-generation status, Pell eligibility) are **not used as model inputs**. They are used only for fairness auditing.

## Output

A calibrated probability in [0, 1] of returning for year 2, plus a `risk_score = 1 - probability` and a four-level band (Low / Moderate / Elevated / High) for advisor triage.

## Training data

- 5,000 synthetic FTIC students across cohort years 2020-2023.
- Generated by `etl/generate_data.py` with a documented data-generating process.
- 75/25 stratified train/test split, seeded for reproducibility.

## Algorithm

- `XGBClassifier` (depth 4, 400 trees, lr 0.06) wrapped in `CalibratedClassifierCV(method="isotonic", cv=3)`.
- Numeric passthrough; one-hot encoding for `program_code`.

## Performance (test set)

Reported automatically in `artifacts/metrics.json` when the model trains. Typical values on the synthetic data:

- ROC-AUC ~ 0.78
- PR-AUC ~ 0.88
- Brier ~ 0.16

## Fairness audit

`artifacts/fairness_audit.csv` reports per-group n, actual retention rate, predicted positive rate, selection rate at risk, ROC-AUC, FPR, and FNR for each value of `gender`, `race_ethnicity`, `first_generation`, and `pell_eligible`. Inspect for parity gaps before any operational use.

## Known limitations

- **Synthetic data.** All relationships in the data are stipulated by the generator; performance figures do not transfer to a real cohort.
- **Construct validity.** "Engagement" is approximated by LMS logins and advising meetings — incomplete proxies.
- **Sample sizes.** Some race/ethnicity subgroups have < 200 students; subgroup metrics are noisy.
- **Concept drift.** A real model would need scheduled retraining and drift monitoring.
- **Feedback loops.** If risk flags change advisor behavior and that behavior changes outcomes, future training data is no longer i.i.d. with the original distribution.

## Responsible use checklist for production

- [ ] FERPA review of inputs and outputs.
- [ ] Title IX and equity review of subgroup performance gaps.
- [ ] Documented intervention protocol (what advisors do with a flag).
- [ ] Human-in-the-loop required before any consequential action.
- [ ] Monitoring plan for calibration, subgroup parity, and intervention effectiveness.
- [ ] Sunset / re-review date set at deployment.
